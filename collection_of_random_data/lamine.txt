Lamine Yamal Nasraoui Ebana, born on 13 July 2007 in Esplugues de Llobregat, Spain, is a Spanish professional footballer who plays as a right winger or right midfielder for La Liga club Barcelona and the Spain national team.
 He is known for his flair, chance creation, and long-distance curling goals, and is widely regarded as one of the best players in the world.
 Yamal, who has a Moroccan father and an Equatorial Guinean mother, grew up in the working-class neighborhood of Rocafonda, where he began playing football at age four with local club CF La Torreta before joining Barcelona's famed La Masia youth academy in 2014.

Yamal made his professional debut for Barcelona at just 15 years, 9 months, and 16 days old on 29 April 2023, becoming the youngest player in the club's history to appear in a competitive match.
 He also became the youngest player to represent Spain at the senior level and the youngest goalscorer for the national team at age 16.
 His performances earned him a nomination for the Ballon d'Or at age 17, making him the youngest nominee in history, and he won the Kopa Trophy for best young player in the world in both 2024 and 2025.

In the 2023–24 season, Yamal broke multiple records, including becoming the youngest player to score in La Liga and the youngest to record an assist in the competition.
 He played a key role in Barcelona's domestic treble during the 2024–25 season under coach Hansi Flick, scoring 18 goals and providing 21 assists, and became the youngest player to make 100 appearances for the club.
 He also became the youngest goalscorer in a Clásico match and the youngest player to appear in a major tournament final during Spain's UEFA Euro 2024 campaign, where Spain won their fourth title.
